## 1. Core philosophy

Settlements emerge from:

`resource access + infrastructure + water + security + economic opportunity`

---

## 2. Lifecycle

`camp → village → town → city → metropolis`  
or collapse / reduction.

---

## 3. Growth drivers

| Driver | Effect |
|:---|:---|
| Rail access | Rapid growth |
| Power availability | Industrialization |
| Water security | Sustainability |
| Defense | Retention |
| Employment / migration | Demographics |

---

## 4. Decline drivers

| Driver | Effect |
|:---|:---|
| War | Displacement |
| Drought | Depopulation |
| Blackout | Economic collapse |
| Pollution / health | Decline |
| Isolation | Stagnation |

---

## 5. Urban sprawl

Preferential growth:

- Along transport corridors, near utilities  
- On stable terrain, near industry  

---

## 6. Migration

Influenced by jobs, safety, housing, climate, food availability.

---

## 7. Informal settlements

Rapid growth may produce slums, shortages, instability, sanitation pressure.

---

## 8. Strategic urbanization

Faction policies may subsidize growth, relocate industry, decentralize cities, fortify regions.

---

## 9. Ecological interaction

Urbanization impacts fire risk, flood runoff, habitat fragmentation, pollution.

---

## 10. Dynamic adaptation

Cities adapt to bombardment, flooding, industrial collapse, climate shifts, infrastructure failures.

---

## 11. Acceptance (v1 drafting)

- [ ] Demographic state has **clear inputs** (food, power, jobs) — no orphan population counters.  
- [ ] Migration pushes/pulls are **debuggable** at coarse LOD first.  
- [ ] Ecology hooks **read** fire/flood/weather overlays without breaking terrain ontology ownership.  

---

## 12. Long-term goal

Settlements become **evolving socio-economic organisms** shaped by infrastructure, conflict, ecology, and logistics.
